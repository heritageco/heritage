"We bring together deep fieldwork expertise and meticulous research methodology to deliver results that stand up to both scholarly and regulatory scrutiny."

— Shelly Heritage Archaeology

Shelly Heritage Archaeology is an independent consulting organization dedicated to the study, documentation, and preservation of the archaeological record across the United States.

We serve a broad spectrum of clients — from federal agencies and state preservation offices to private developers, landowners, and nonprofits — guiding each project from initial reconnaissance through final reporting with clarity and precision.

Our team brings rigorous field methods to every engagement, combining archival depth with on-the-ground expertise. We understand both the scholarly stakes and the regulatory landscape that governs cultural resource work in the United States.

Whether your project is a federal undertaking subject to Section 106 or a private development seeking due diligence, we provide the expertise to move forward confidently.

What We Offer

Core
Services

From desk-based historical research through full Phase III data recovery, we offer end-to-end archaeological consulting that meets the highest professional and regulatory standards. Hover any service to learn more.

01
Historical Site Research

Comprehensive desk-based assessments integrating USGS data, historical maps, archival records, and prior survey reports to identify archaeological potential before fieldwork begins.

02
Phase I–III Excavation

Full-service field investigations from Phase I identification surveys through Phase II evaluation and Phase III data recovery, executed with precision and thorough documentation.

03
Cultural Resource Management

End-to-end CRM support for federal undertakings, private development, and infrastructure projects — ensuring compliance and protecting significant cultural resources.

04
Artifact Preservation

On-site stabilization, laboratory processing, and conservation assessment of recovered materials. We follow federal curation guidelines and coordinate with approved repositories.

05
Report Writing & Documentation

Detailed technical reports meeting state and federal standards — from site forms and Phase I survey reports to National Register nominations and MOAs.

06
Public & Private Sector Collaboration

Experienced liaison work between developers, agencies, Tribal Historic Preservation Offices, and SHPOs — facilitating communication and finding workable paths forward.

Our Process

From Research
to Report

01
Scoping & Research

Archival records, site file reviews, map analysis, and regulatory context define scope and strategy before entering the field.

02
Field Investigation

Systematic survey and excavation with rigorous documentation: photo logs, stratigraphic profiles, and GPS-registered find locations.

03
Analysis & Consultation

Laboratory processing, specialist analysis, and consultation with SHPOs, THPOs, and federal agencies at each stage.

04
Reporting & Delivery

Final technical reports prepared to the highest professional standards — clear, compliant, and delivered on schedule.
